What Buffering Strategy is right for me?

Jul 25, 2015 by djherbis

Buffering is useful for a lot of projects, however every task has different requirements and therefore deserves it’s own buffering strategy.

Buffering in its simplest form is easy, store some bytes which will be read later. It gets harder when you start adding limited resources and other contraints to the problem.

Buffering can be divided into two categories: Bounded & Unbounded.

Bounded Buffering

Bounded buffers are much safer since you can control how much of your resources they take up, however you need to decide what happens to your dataflows when they do.

Blocking Buffers

The simplest answer here is: block until there’s room. In this case you are guarenteed not to lose data, however Write’s to the buffer will block until some data is Read.

However there are cases where blocking isn’t what you want. Sometimes losing data is better than clogging the pipe.

Non-Blocking Buffers

Overflow: data coming in is thrown away when the buffer is full. Ring: the oldest data in the buffer is thrown away to make room for newer data.

Unbounded Buffering

Unbounded buffers are almost never a good idea, because in reality we never have unlimited resources on which to store them. However I didn’t want to preclude users from being able to shoot themselves in the foot if their boss asks them too so I’ve included ways to create buffers which can grow forever.
